The construction phase has taken place over a few different days, with other things getting in the way. The fear of pressing the seams in wrong took over for a good week too, and it sat there staring at me pointedly. This morning I got over that, and a little while later we have the major part of the construction done.

Bits and Pieces

The first thing was to sew a seam at the shoulders on both the lining and the "outer". Once this was done, I could really see the shape that was coming up.

The back of me

Once this was done, the real fun began. Stitching the front to the lining, and turning out. I should have takenÂ  a photo of it half turned out, where the entire thing went through the shoulder, it was impressive!

There are two places that are not yet sewn up, although I'm not sure if it's supposed to be one place. Anyway, onto pressing in those seams...

Pressing seams - Please ignore my shoe!

So now it's looking something like the correct shape. I say something like, the lining is very slippery material, and things have gotten a little out of joint. Now just ready for that last pair of seams to be stitched together. If project #1 is anything to go by, this will be the most harrowing part. Wish me luck!
