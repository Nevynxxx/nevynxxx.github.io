Hello! I thought my first post should be about my first project. I really am just beginning with all this sewing malarkey so I decided to do something simple that would help me get used to the sewing machine.

I started with a free pdf pattern for a "Monster" and set to one evening with some off-cuts of fabric from some of Bec's projects. I got to pinning and cutting and then stitching on the eyes and things. I didn't fancy trying tight circles as my first go on the machine, so I did the eyes and things by hand with some sparkly thread I found. Then to the main event.

First I sewed a few practice runs on some off cuts to work out the stitch I wanted for the mouth. I then proceeded to sew the mouth at entirely the wrong angle. Well, it is just a practice piece. Next time I think I'll have to draw a line on to follow, rather than trying to free hand a curve.

Next was sewing the pieces together. This was about the time I worked out that I hadn't been as careful as I thought with my cutting, and the was a big V chunk out of one of the arms. Thing to remember #2.

The actual stitching went easily enough. Although I didn't leave quite a big enough gap to turn it out, so had to unpick a little bit to get it tuned around. Once it was turned out I also found I'd gone a bit close to the edge, and the seams were not too tight. They looked like they'd start to come apart. For the worst one, I turned it back and re-did with the machine. For the others I went over them by hand just to firm them up.

Finally a bit of over-stuffing, and hand stitched the last part of the seam.

All in all this took about two hours, and I learned quite a bit. The pattern is really good, but I did find the hands and feet should be a bit bigger, they have come out a little bit small and they were really difficult to stuff properly.

The final result? Given to my wife (and stolen by my daughter!)
